    Information to Recruiters

    I get a high volume of inboud messages about new job opportunities. I don't switch jobs without a good reason, and don't have enough free time to engage with multiple hiring processes. In addition, many contacts are either not relevant for me (wrong tech stack, wrong experience level...), or simply don't provide enough useful information to decide if it's a worthy opportunity.

    To filter signal from noise, I've established inbound messages rules that I require anyone sending me job opportunities to follow. If you're sending me a job opportunity without complying with those rules, that tells me you're spamming me without having done your homework, so your message will be ignored.

    These are the rules for inbound messages about job oportunities:

    • Salary range required: it's a waste of time for both parties to go through the process just to find later that it's a salary downgrade
    • Senior or leadership positions only: if someone is presenting me a mid-level job, it tells me they haven't even skimmed my CV before contacting me
    • Remote only, ideally with flexible schedule, and no relocation: This is how I can deliver my best work

    You can find an updated version of my CV and contact me on LinkedIn.

    I will try to reply to every contact that follows these rules, even if just to say that I'm not looking for job changes at that moment.

    Thank you for your understanding!
